Johnson KABERA

Johnson Kabera is the Managing Partner at Kigali Allied Advocates. Before joining Kigali Allied Advocates, Johnson worked as a prosecutor for a period of six years starting the year 2000. He is a Fellow (FCIARB) of the Chartered Institute of Arbitrators, London.

He holds a Bachelor`s Degree in Law from the National University of Rwanda obtained in 1999 He has attended many trainings and obtained different certificates including a Certificate in International Trade and Development (Trade Policy Training Centre for Africa, Arusha, Tanzania May 2010, Certificate course for the Associate membership of the Chartered Institute of Arbitrators, 2011 and many others in the Justice Sector. He is involved in training prosecutors and judges at the Institute of Legal Practice and Development in Nyanza as a trainer in different disciplines but especially the Gender –based Violence, professional ethics of advocates and pleading techniques.

More to his experience in criminal matters from his work as a prosecutor, he is very familiar with labour laws and between July and Dec 2009 he was hired as one of the consultants to draft 15 Ministerial Orders to implement the Law regulating labour in Rwanda. He is also a specialist in arbitration a field he got interested in and obtained trainings. He has participated in high profile arbitral tribunals either as counsel or as a party appointed arbitrator.

He is a member of the trainings and the ethics committee of Kigali Bar Association and also Member of the East African Law Society. He is fluent in English Kinyarwanda and a working knowledge of French

Frank MUBANGIZI

Frank MUBANGIZI is a private lawyer of eight years of experience. He obtained his Bachelor of Laws degree (LLB) from the National University of Rwanda in 1999, he is a member of the Rwanda Bar Association and has been in Private Law Practice since early 2007.

He is a partner at Kigali Allied Advocates. Prior to joining the Law firm Frank MUBANGIZI worked with the Ministry of Justice in Legislation department in 1999 and later became a Public prosecutor for a period of six years. Frank also worked with United Nations Stabilization Mission in Haiti (MINUNSTA) as a judicial affairs officer.

He has worked as lead counsel for clients in labor, Civil and commercial litigations in Rwandan courts of Law. He is a native speaker of Kinyarwanda, has excellent command of English and a working knowledge of French and Swahili.

He is a member of the Rwanda Bar Association East African Law Society (EALS) and one of the founder members of Youth Association for the Promotion of Human Rights Frank has attended many trainings and obtained different certificates including a Certificate in Leadership skills and Human Rights among others.

Jacqueline BAKAMURERA

Ms. Jacqueline Bakamurera is a Partner with Kigali Allied Advocates. Before joining the Bar, she was the Assistant Attorney General in the Ministry of Justice in Charge of Community Programs Human Rights and Legal Aid Services which she headed since October 2007.

Prior to that, she was the President of the Intermediate Court of Rubavu in the Western Province a position she held since 2004. Before being posted in Rubavu she had served in the same capacity in the Province of the East from 2001. During that time, she was a Judge, she was instrumental in adjudicating certain genocide cases and also giving advice on Human Rights issues and on the amendment of certain laws notably the Rwandan Penal Code, Family Code and other laws.

She holds a Bachelor of Laws degree (LLB) from the National University of Rwanda, Butare and a Diploma in Legal Practice from ILPD. Jacqueline is a native speaker of Kinyarwanda, has excellent command of English and a working knowledge of French.
